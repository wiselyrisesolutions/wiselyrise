Frequently Asked Questions

What makes KeyWise different from other password managers? ▼

KeyWise is built on zero-knowledge architecture, meaning your credentials are encrypted on-device before sync and backup. We use Argon2id key derivation and XChaCha20-Poly1305 authenticated encryption. Unlike cloud-dependent solutions, KeyWise works offline-first, so you always have access to your vaults. We don't ask for payment upfront for basic features—the free tier is genuinely useful.

Can I access my vaults offline? ▼

Yes. KeyWise is offline-first by design. All your credentials are stored locally on your device, encrypted with your master password. You can access them anytime, anywhere—with or without internet. Cloud backup and sync are optional premium features for convenience.

How secure is my data? ▼

Your data is protected by military-grade encryption. We use XChaCha20-Poly1305 for authenticated encryption and Argon2id for key derivation. Your encryption keys never leave your device—not even WiselyRise can decrypt your data. Every credential is encrypted before storage, and the only way to access it is with your master password, which only you know.

What if I forget my master password? ▼

Your master password is the only key to decrypt your vault—we cannot recover it for you. This is a feature, not a bug: it ensures nobody (not even us) can access your data. However, we provide a recovery code during setup that lets you regain access if you forget your password. Keep it safe in a secure location.

Can I switch devices? ▼

Yes. You can export your vault to our encrypted .keywise format, which includes all credentials and is protected by your master password. Transfer this file to your new device and restore it. Your data is always portable and under your control.

Does KeyWise have a web app? ▼

Currently, KeyWise is available as a native Android app. This design choice keeps your data on your device and avoids browser security risks. You can sync and back up your vault to cloud storage (premium feature), but viewing always happens on your device for maximum security.
