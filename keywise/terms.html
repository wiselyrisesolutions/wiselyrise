Description of Service

KeyWise is a personal and family credential manager for Android (and iOS) devices. It allows users to:

  • Store and organise login credentials, PINs, passwords, and other private credentials across multiple vaults.
  • Use creative, non-shoulder-surfable lock methods (Constellation Draw, Memory Mosaic, Story Sequence, Gesture Chord, Voice Passphrase) to protect vault access.
  • Manage credentials for multiple family members and identities through a multi-vault structure.
  • Check credentials against known breach databases using the Have I Been Pwned k-anonymity API.
  • Autofill credentials on Android via the Autofill Service framework.
  • Store TOTP two-factor authentication secrets alongside credentials.
  • Sync an encrypted vault across up to 5 devices and maintain automatic encrypted backups (Premium tier).
  • Share vault access with family members and designate a trusted contact for emergency access (Premium tier).

The App operates in an offline-first mode — all vault data is stored locally in an encrypted database on your device. Internet connectivity is used only for breach checks, cloud sync (Premium), and purchase verification (Premium).

Security Responsibilities

⚠️ Important. KeyWise uses a zero-knowledge architecture. This means WiselyRise cannot read your vault, reset your lock credential, or recover your data on your behalf. The security obligations in this section are yours alone.

6.1 Your Lock Credential

Your creative lock — whether a constellation pattern, photo sequence, story order, gesture chord, or voice passphrase — is the sole input to your vault's encryption key. You are solely responsible for remembering and protecting your lock credential. WiselyRise has no record of it and cannot help you recover it if it is forgotten.

6.2 Your Recovery Code

During setup, KeyWise generates a 24-word BIP-39 Recovery Code. This is the only mechanism for recovering vault access if your lock credential is lost or your device is replaced. You must:

  • Write down your Recovery Code and store it in a physically secure location (such as a locked drawer or safe).
  • Never store your Recovery Code digitally in plaintext or in the same device as KeyWise.
  • Never share your Recovery Code with anyone, including WiselyRise support staff.
🔑 If both your lock credential and your Recovery Code are lost, your vault is permanently irrecoverable. This is not a limitation — it is the security guarantee. WiselyRise has no backdoor and no master key.

6.3 Device Security

You are responsible for maintaining the physical security of the device on which KeyWise is installed. If your device is lost, stolen, or accessed by an unauthorised person, they cannot access your KeyWise vault without your lock credential — but you should change any credentials stored in KeyWise for any service where your device was also a registered authentication factor.

6.4 Trusted Contact & Emergency Access — Premium

If you designate a Trusted Contact for emergency vault access (Premium feature), you accept that this person will be able to request access to your encrypted vault after a 72-hour notification window. You are responsible for choosing a Trusted Contact whose judgement and intentions you trust. WiselyRise does not verify the identity of Trusted Contacts beyond the App's built-in confirmation flow.

6.5 Autofill Service

When the Android Autofill Service is enabled, KeyWise suggests credentials on matching apps and websites. You are responsible for verifying that credentials are being filled into legitimate, expected applications and not into malicious apps designed to mimic trusted services.

No Security Guarantee

KeyWise is a tool to help you organise and protect your credentials. It is not a guarantee of security. In particular:

  • Breach check results (via Have I Been Pwned) indicate whether a password appears in known breach datasets. A password not listed in HIBP may still be compromised through unreported breaches, targeted attacks, or future incidents. Treat breach check results as a useful signal, not a security certificate.
  • Reset prediction estimates when a portal may force a password reset based on historical patterns. These predictions are informational and may be inaccurate for any given portal or time period.
  • Portal templates encode known login methods and field labels for common services. These templates may become outdated as services change their login flows. Always verify that a template matches the current state of the portal.
  • Password strength indicators are based on general heuristic rules. A password rated as strong may still be cracked given sufficient resources or if it has been reused elsewhere.

You remain solely responsible for the security practices you adopt and for any consequences arising from the credentials you store in KeyWise.
