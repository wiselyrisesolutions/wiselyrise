Privacy Policy

How KeyWise handles your data — in plain English.

Last updated: 7 June 2026
TL;DR — The short version
  • Your credentials are encrypted on your device before anything happens — we never see your vault contents.
  • The Free tier requires no account, no email, no cloud — zero data leaves your phone.
  • The HIBP breach check uses k-anonymity — only a 5-character hash prefix is sent, never your actual password.
  • Premium cloud sync only ever receives unintelligible encrypted blobs — not credentials, not usernames, not URLs.
  • Voice passphrase and Memory Mosaic photo data are processed entirely on-device — never uploaded.
  • We do not sell, share, or monetise any user data. Ever.
01

Introduction

KeyWise ("the App", "we", "our") is developed and maintained by WiselyRise Solutions ("WiselyRise", "us"). This Privacy Policy explains how KeyWise collects, uses, stores, and protects your information when you use the KeyWise mobile application on Android (and iOS).

KeyWise is built on a zero-knowledge, offline-first architecture. Your vault data is encrypted on your device using keys derived from your personal lock credential before it is ever processed, synced, or backed up. WiselyRise is cryptographically unable to read your credentials — not by policy alone, but by design.

By using KeyWise, you agree to the practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please discontinue use of the App.

02

Information We Collect

2.1 Vault & Credential Data

All data you enter in KeyWise — including login credentials, usernames, passwords, PINs, URLs, portal templates, notes, TOTP secrets, login method tags, and credential history — is stored exclusively in an AES-256 encrypted local database (SQLCipher) on your device.

🔒 Free tier users: Your vault data is never transmitted to WiselyRise or any third party. It never leaves your device. We have no visibility into what credentials you store, which portals you use, or any personal details.
☁️ Premium tier users: If you enable cloud sync, your encrypted vault blob is transmitted to Firebase Cloud Firestore. The blob is encrypted client-side before transmission — Firebase (and WiselyRise) receive ciphertext only. See §4.4 and §5.2 for details.

2.2 Creative Lock & Biometric Data

KeyWise's five creative lock methods — Constellation Draw, Memory Mosaic (personal photos), Story Sequence, Gesture Chord, and Voice Passphrase — all process authentication data exclusively on your device.

🖼️ Memory Mosaic — Personal Photos

During setup, you select up to 12 photos from your device gallery as your lock sequence. These photos are stored locally in the App's encrypted container. They are never uploaded, transmitted, or analysed by WiselyRise. The App accesses your photo library only at the moment you explicitly choose photos during lock setup.

🎙️ Voice Passphrase

During enrolment, three voice recordings are used to compute a local voice embedding (a mathematical representation of your voice). The recording is processed entirely on-device; only the derived embedding is stored locally. No audio recordings and no voice embeddings are ever uploaded or shared.

Standard biometric authentication (fingerprint, face unlock) is handled entirely by the Android Biometric API — your device's operating system. KeyWise never accesses, stores, or transmits raw biometric data.

2.3 Have I Been Pwned — Breach Check

KeyWise's credential health score includes a breach check that queries Have I Been Pwned (HIBP) using the k-anonymity model:

K-anonymity protocol — what is sent SHA-1(your_password) → e.g. "5BAA61..." Only the first 5 hex chars → "5BAA6" are sent to HIBP. HIBP returns ~500 matching hash suffixes. KeyWise checks locally whether your full hash is in the list. Your actual password is never sent — not even partially.

This request contains no personal data, no account identifiers, and no IP attribution. It is governed by HIBP's Privacy Policy. If your device is offline, the breach check is skipped and the health score reflects only locally assessable criteria.

2.4 Cloud Sync & Backup — Premium Only

Premium tier users may optionally enable:

Firebase Cloud Firestore — Device Sync

Allows your vault to stay in sync across up to 5 devices. Firestore receives only encrypted binary blobs. The encryption key is derived from your personal lock credential using Argon2id on your device — Firestore (and WiselyRise) have no access to the key and cannot decrypt the data.

Google Drive — Automatic Backup

Stores up to 5 timestamped encrypted vault backups in your personal Google Drive (App Data folder, not visible in your Drive files). Google Drive receives the same client-side encrypted blob as Firestore. Your Google credentials are handled by your device's Google Sign-In — KeyWise never sees your Google password.

💡 Both cloud features are opt-in. They can be disabled at any time from App Settings → Cloud & Backup. Disabling sync does not delete existing cloud data; you must manually clear it from the App Settings if desired.

2.5 Purchase Verification — Premium Only

When you purchase the KeyWise Premium one-time unlock, purchase verification is handled via RevenueCat, a third-party purchase management platform. RevenueCat processes your purchase receipt from Google Play to verify your entitlement. RevenueCat may receive:

What RevenueCat receives

A pseudonymous installation identifier, your Google Play purchase receipt (managed by Google), and an entitlement status. RevenueCat does not receive your vault data, your credentials, or your lock method. See RevenueCat's Privacy Policy.

2.6 Crash Reports & Analytics

KeyWise does not currently integrate any analytics, telemetry, or crash-reporting SDK (such as Firebase Crashlytics, Sentry, or Mixpanel). No usage data or crash logs are transmitted to WiselyRise.

If this changes in a future version, this policy will be updated and users will be informed via an in-app notice and Play Store update notes before the change takes effect.

2.7 Information We Do NOT Collect

  • Your name, email address, phone number, or any contact information (Free tier)
  • Location data — GPS or IP-based
  • Advertising identifiers (GAID/IDFA)
  • Browsing history, app usage patterns, or behavioural data
  • Contacts, SMS, or call logs
  • The content of your vault — credentials, passwords, PINs, notes
  • Voice recordings or photo data from your creative lock
  • Any data from users on the Free tier beyond what the App needs to function locally
03

How We Use Your Information

Because WiselyRise does not receive your vault data, we have no use for it. All credential processing happens on your device.

The limited data we do receive (for Premium users) is used solely for the following purposes:

Firebase Firestore — Encrypted Vault Sync

Used exclusively to synchronise your encrypted vault across your authorised devices. The data is never read, analysed, or shared.

Google Drive — Encrypted Backup

Used exclusively to store versioned encrypted backups in your personal Google Drive App Data folder for recovery purposes.

RevenueCat — Purchase Receipt

Used exclusively to verify your one-time Premium purchase and restore your entitlement on a new device. Not used for any marketing or profiling.

HIBP Breach Check — Hash Prefix

Used exclusively to check whether any of your stored passwords appear in known breach databases, and to compute your credential health score. Not stored, not logged, not linked to your identity.

04

Data Storage & Security

4.1 Zero-Knowledge Encryption Architecture

KeyWise implements a verifiable zero-knowledge architecture. Your vault encryption key is derived exclusively from your personal lock credential — it never touches WiselyRise servers. The cryptographic specification is public and auditable:

Encryption stack Lock credential (constellation / photo / gesture / voice / story) ↓ Argon2id KDF (memory: 64 MB, iterations: 3, parallelism: 4) Vault Encryption Key (VEK) — 256-bit, derived on-device ↓ XChaCha20-Poly1305 AEAD cipher Encrypted vault blob — stored locally (SQLCipher AES-256) ↓ For Premium sync only Uploaded to Firestore / Google Drive as opaque ciphertext

Uninstalling the App destroys the local database and all associated keys. Your vault is irrecoverable by WiselyRise — not as a policy choice, but because the keys never exist anywhere other than your device.

4.2 Creative Lock Security

All five creative lock methods (Constellation Draw, Memory Mosaic, Story Sequence, Gesture Chord, Voice Passphrase) derive a deterministic passphrase that is fed into Argon2id. The lock method is the input layer — the cryptographic pipeline is identical regardless of which method you choose. None of the lock inputs are stored in plaintext.

4.3 App Lock & Recovery

The App enforces a progressive cooldown after failed unlock attempts — no sudden lockouts. A 30-day soft-delete window applies to deleted credentials, after which they are permanently purged from the local database.

The Recovery Code (24 BIP-39 mnemonic words, generated at setup) is the only key recovery mechanism. It is displayed once and never transmitted or stored by WiselyRise. If both the lock credential and Recovery Code are lost, the vault is irrecoverable by design — zero-knowledge is maintained absolutely.

The Trusted Contact emergency access feature (Premium) allows a designated family member to request access to your vault after a 72-hour notification window. This process uses the same encrypted channel as cloud sync — WiselyRise cannot intercept or read the vault during emergency access.

4.4 Cloud Sync Security — Premium

Your Firebase and Google Drive sync data consists entirely of encrypted vault blobs produced on your device. Firebase rules are configured so that only an authenticated device associated with your purchase can read or write your encrypted data. WiselyRise engineers cannot read your synced vault.

Children's Privacy

KeyWise is not directed to children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. The Family Vault feature is intended for use by a parent or guardian who manages credentials on behalf of a minor — the minor's own data is stored locally under the parent's vault and subject to this same policy.

If you believe a child under 13 has provided personal information through the App, please contact us at contact@wiselyrise.in and we will take immediate steps to address it.

07

Data Retention

Free tier: All data is stored locally on your device. Retention is entirely in your control. Uninstalling the App permanently removes all associated data from your device.

Premium tier — Cloud Sync: Encrypted vault data stored in Firebase Firestore is retained for as long as you maintain an active Premium entitlement. You may delete your cloud data at any time from App Settings → Cloud & Backup → Delete Cloud Data. Upon deletion, the encrypted blob is removed from Firestore within 30 days in accordance with Firebase's data deletion policies.

Premium tier — Google Drive Backup: Encrypted backup files are stored in your personal Google Drive App Data folder. You can view and delete these files via Google Drive settings (Storage → App Data → KeyWise).

Deleted credentials: A soft-deleted credential remains in the local recycle bin for 30 days before permanent purge. Cloud sync reflects the purge on the next sync cycle.

RevenueCat: Purchase receipts are retained by RevenueCat for the duration required to support entitlement restore on new devices, in accordance with their data retention policy.
